## Project Types

Two categories appear across projects:

- **Exterior elevations** — contemporary residential façade designs; emphasis on materiality (stone, wood, jaali panels, glass), light interaction, and volumetric composition
- **Interior design** — double-height living volumes, bespoke furniture, false ceilings, layered lighting, bedroom retreats

## Firm Design Philosophy

Volume 9's descriptions follow a consistent voice and set of values:

- **Warm modernism**: minimal forms softened by natural materials (stone, timber, textured surfaces)
- **Light as architecture**: lighting integrated into the design narrative, not added as an afterthought
- **Material restraint**: small palettes chosen for harmony and longevity, not contrast
- **Spatial experience**: spaces are described in terms of how they feel and unfold, not just how they look
- **Timeless over trendy**: designs framed as enduring rather than fashionable

When writing or editing project descriptions, maintain this voice: evocative but restrained, design-literate, first-person plural avoided, no marketing superlatives.

## Description File Format

Project descriptions typically include:
1. One-paragraph overview (project identity, key design moves)
2. Optional narrative sections with headers (`## *Section Name*`) covering: Vision, Form, Materiality, Light, Spatial sequence, Detail/Craft
3. Closing statement referencing Volume 9's philosophy

Tone: architectural prose, present tense, active voice. Avoid: "luxury", "state-of-the-art", "world-class", generic adjectives.
